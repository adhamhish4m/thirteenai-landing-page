where to start

Start with what you hate doing.

Not with the tech. Never with the tech. Start with the jobs that eat your week and drain you, then work backwards. These are the ones small businesses hand over first.

Missed and after-hours calls

The phone rings while you're on a job or asleep, and it goes to voicemail. That's a lost customer. AI can pick up, screen it, and book it.

Following up with leads

Someone enquires, you get busy, and you never reply. Most sales are lost in the follow-up. This runs itself so nothing slips.

Booking and reminders

Back-and-forth to find a time, then no-shows when they forget. Automated booking and reminders quietly fix both.

Invoicing and admin

The work's done but the invoice waits for a free afternoon that never comes. Finished jobs can turn into invoices on their own.

The same questions, over and over

Hours a week answering the same handful of questions. An assistant that knows your business can handle them for you.

Copying data between tools

Retyping the same details into a spreadsheet, then your inbox, then your CRM. Nobody misses this one. It's the easiest to hand over.

The pattern is always the same. Find the task you dread. The one that's repetitive, eats time, and honestly doesn't need you.

That's where you start. One at a time. Not some big scary transformation. Just one job off your plate, then the next.
